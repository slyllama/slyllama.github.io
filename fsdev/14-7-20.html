Other Links of Interest

  • Fuel Air Mixture – AOPA. A good, and in-depth write-up. Here are some of the notes I took that we need to be especially aware of:
    • The engine should be leaned as far as possible (within reason) for taxiing and other ground operations. It should then be full rich for take-off.
    • Mixture checks happen roughly from 5000ft, and every 2000ft thereafter. Starting from full rich, slowly lean the engine and observe the tachometer. If the RPM jumps up, the engine was probably too rich, and you can settle on a little leaner.
