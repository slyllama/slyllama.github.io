Breadboard Basics

I’ve always been meaning to learn about designing circuits on a breadboard, and for me, the best way to learn is to observe a quality source and make proper notes of my own. I found this video from Simply Electronics (looks like they haven’t updated in a while, unfortunately) which was short, clear, and instructive. So, without further ado, here is my reference diagram featuring an abnormally small breadboard:

  • (a) shows the layout of the sockets themselves. The top two rows are “power rails” for positive and negative power. They are electrically common; connecting the end-most sockets to a power source will activate all of the sockets for that line.
  • The bulk of the units are vertically common; each column is linked up, and these are then chained using jumper leads.
  • (b) demonstrates the circuit shown in the video: a simple LED with resistor. (I really hope I’ve gotten these details right…)
