Toki Pona and Dwelt

As a more portable hobby, I’ve been working on my Toki Pona (toki pona). The language features a small number of words each with a wide semantic space. (“lon lon”, for instance, could mean ‘[being] at’, ‘truth’, and ‘reality’). Toki Pona has a couple of writing systems – one of them is called “sitelen sitelen sitelen sitelen”, the one shown above and on the left. It’s a very spatial way of writing out language where the properties of things, for instance, are physically contained next to those things. I like to see it as a lunch-box, or a bunch of oddly square animal friends. (nimi.li is a great Toki Pona resource where you can click on any word and get the gist of its semantic space.)

And lastly for now, I’ve been starting to look into settling on and developing my next game in addition to continuing to work on Jade Spring!! It will be quite small, but wholly original. Something with a mix of robots and magical spaces, combat and exploration. I’ve had a lot of ideas about a setting called Dwelt for many years, and now I might be kinda ready to depict at least a little bit of that world and feeling in some small way. Stay tuned!
