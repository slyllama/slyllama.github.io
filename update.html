Changes to “Llamaville”

2nd March, 2020

Hello everyone! I’ve been really appreciating this comfy space we have, but I’ve left it a little quiet since finishing school. To be honest, I’ve been creatively lost since January, and my engagment has reflected that. However, I do have some new visions and projects in mind, and I’d like to refresh the space to correspond accordingly as we go forward and make new things. So without further ado, here are the changes I want to make:

Server changes

Channel Changes Description (if changed)
#welcome Updated to describe the following changes accurately. -
#inventions Renamed to #constructions. This term can better cover creations which represent an assembly of parts. “A home for any creative effort from any of us: music; illustration; writing; whatever comes to mind!”
#thingistry My belief is that #thingistry (T) and #inspiration (I) cover similar topics, but T ended up being far less used. For this reason, I am going to archive T onto the Sly Llama website, and rename I to T. “A room for all of the weird and wonderful creative things you really like.”
#stellaris Will be removed. I intend to put an archive of my thesis on the website instead, which records everything posted to this channel. -
#guides Will be removed. These topics are already well-represented by the site! -
  • Moderators will no longer be forced to be ‘Janis’-labeled anymore (unless this is something that they deeply desire).
  • We might open petitions for another server icon! It would be nice to have a cycle going…

Website changes

I have a website which is currently being used to host the architectural illustration guides we have developed over the years. I’d like to make it a little more general-purpose, with a new focus on story-telling. For this reason, I’m going to move the guides into a /guides sub-folder, Stellaris will be represented under a /thesis directory, and I’ll be working on the home page and auxiliary pages to accomodate the new efforts I want to put time into, described below. I’m no developer, so these pages will likely be simple and old-fashioned (like this one)!

A personal note

I deeply appreciate all of the support that you have given me over the years! It really warms my heart. I’m not quite sure where I’m going next, but I know that I want to work with words, sound, and music (perhaps with some illustrative ornament); something much more audio oriented. I have stories I want to tell, and it’s just a case of finding the most natural fit for them. Thank you again for being with me. ❤

—Sly Llama, 2020
