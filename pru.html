If the family is a tree, then the spirit of Prudence lingers at the very boundaries of its sprawling roots, tapping gently through fresh soil and navigating between the dense veins of clay dispersed about the fertile earth. In this manner does she invisibly encircle her kin, as well as all of those companions with whom they is connected: a silver phantom balancing on the wire that hazily divides exploration and interiority.

Prudence has always been blind, unable to perceive an unfiltered plane: but through the use of charged sight-glasses she is able to observe the currents of both predestination and chaos in the vicinity of her circular world. (Think of such an act as a computer accepting huge streams of binary and using its internal filters and rules to rapidly make sense of this information.)

You will likely never see her, but one can feel her presence brush past, as sleek and silvery as her tidy fur. And, at your time of greatest need – tears forecasting the dire storms – she will be on your lap, soft heartbeat synchronised with the rhythm of planes in union.
