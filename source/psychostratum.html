The craft of sub-creation is a vital, beautiful, and substantive one. To witness the sculpture of fictive characters—a craftsperson stirring up and consolidating the milieu of histories, aspirations, victories, set-backs, and emotions that crystallise out of the broth of human experience and imagination into characters who come alive in print and performance—is to behold chaotic, passionate machinations which reveal the nature of things, brushing against or colliding with what it means to be human; what it means to live.
