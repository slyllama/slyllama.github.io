Slyllama

Fragments of One

Amidst the rocky pools of silver mist,
gentle boots sit steady on sound plateaus.
Pausing, head raised; lantern elevated as platinum
is cast in veins through weathered fog.
At his feet streak wispy orbs of light, chirping
and streaming and bouncing and laughing—
and so he laughs in harmony, chest softly shaking
as surrounding sprouts soak up golden rain.

Discerned possessions

At One's locus stands a tall, supple staff of about five feet. Fashioned from wood which bears neither twist nor taper, the carved texture of the branch has been finished with a smooth silver of polish. It is capped at its height by a cylinder of beaten metal to which is welded a triangular arc, terminating in a handsome lantern of pewter and warmly distorted glass. Across the face of this assembly have been embossed curving designs which intermingle playfully with one another. This staff represents the most fully crystallised landmark of One's home, forming the cornerstone of his personal sanctuary in lieu of any permanent dwelling. As he bears his lantern and lifts it high, the landscape around him is cast in a warm, silvery glow, and it is through these tones and shadows that he reads the shape of the hills and valleys and charts the course of his ever-changing, never-ceasing journey.
